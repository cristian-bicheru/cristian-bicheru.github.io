What Is This Site?

This site is dedicated to my Who Am I summative project, it will contain lots of information about me. This includes my Myers-Briggs personality type, my learning styles, etc.

Character Quiz

When I took the character quiz, I got that I'm a "Tweety"! This means that I'm loyal, a best friend, witty and calm most of the time, and that people can really count on me. I think I agree with that, I have lots of close friends, and I like to think that I'm always calm! This can help me in school because knowing how other people see me can help me work more efficiently in groups.

Learning Styles

After completing the Learning Styles quiz, I got that I'm a 43% visual learner, 29% auditory learner, and 28% kinesthetic learner. This means that I like to take detailed notes, I like to sit near the front, and I like to turn concepts into visuals, such as charts and diagrams. Using this information, in order to learn and retain information better in school I should underline or highlight key ideas in my notes, make charts and diagrams, and use graphic organizers.

True Colors

The True Colors quiz said that my true color is green. Green people are 'thinkers'. They are self sufficient and independent, rely on logic, and do research/think before making decisions. I can definitely see myself here, I typically won't make important decisions without some thought or research. This will help me in school again because it will help me fit in in group assignments or teamwork settings.

Myers-Briggs Personality

My Myers-Briggs personality code is INTP. This stands for introverted, intuiting, thinking, and perceiving. Some key descriptions of my personality are that I prefer to work in smaller groups, I use intuition when completing tasks, and that I am fair and use my brain when making decisions. In general, knowing my own personality can again help me maximize my contributions when working in a group setting, but it can also help with picking career paths and setting goals.

Multiple Intelligences

The Multiple Intelligences quiz suggested that I have strong logic/math skills, intrapersonal skills, and kinesthetic skills. This means that I enjoy exploring relations between things, enjoy understanding how things work, have a good sense of myself, like to think things over, and that I like to spend time by myself. Additionally, it means I like to move and might be good at sports. This can help me because it greatly affects what career I may choose to pursue and thus it benefits me in school because I can choose courses based on these results to maximize my learning/grades.

Interests

According to the Interests quiz, my interest type is "The Investigator". This means that I enjoy investigative-style jobs. Investigators are typically inquisitive, organized, and have a knack for observing fine details. When you need a detailed, systematic analysis with well-reasoned conclusions, ask an investigator! I think this description does suit me well. I think I would enjoy jobs such as being a data scientist, programmer, or researcher. This knowledge can help me choose the right career path for my future and help me maximize my learning in school, similar to how the Multiple Intelligences quiz can.

Knowledge

The results I got from the Knowledge quiz were Business, Computers, and Mathematics (top 3). This means that I am likely most skilled/interested in those particular fields. I do enjoy those fields a lot! Specifically, I enjoy Business/Law, Computer Science, Mathematics, and Physics. Knowing this will be really helpful since it will directly influence which career path I will take, what courses I will pick, where I focus my studying, etc.

Motivations

From the Motivations quiz, I got that my primary motivation factor is achievement, and my second motivation factor is independence. This means that I'm driven by achievement in my everyday life, and I enjoy doing things alone. I guess I agree with the first part, I love doing well on things and seeing the results, but the second part is only true sometimes. For small projects, I don't like working in big teams since it's inefficient/counter-productive, however in larger projects, teamwork helps a ton. Knowing my motivations is extremely important since it will let me make goals which are easier to achieve (by carefully designing goals such that I have more motivation to complete them).

Transferable Skills

From the Transferable Skills handout, I got that I have strong problem solving and leadership skills. I've become an executive for two clubs this year, these are Mathletes and Computer Science club, and I plan to exec two more (hopefully). This demonstrates that I enjoy and have skills in leadership. I also thoroughly enjoy and practice math and computer science. These fields require lots of problem solving, which has likely developed my problem solving skill set. Knowing my skills will help me make the most of my time in high school, by working on my strengths and improving them.
